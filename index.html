6.Swimming – Schwimmen

Swimming in the Oker and Fümmelsee lake was and is much more than simply physical exercise for many Wolfenbüttel residents. Traditionally, people learned to swim in one of the River Oker's swimming baths. In 1928 alone, the address book of the town of Wolfenbüttel recorded five swimming baths in the municipal area. With the building of the first public swimming pool on the Oker in 1952, that traditional bathing landscape changed. Now people swim in the “Okeraue” municipal pool, but they also continue to do so in the Fümmelsee, one of the last natural swimming lakes in Lower Saxony.
